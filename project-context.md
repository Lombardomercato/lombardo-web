# 1. What Lombardo Is

Lombardo is a meeting place in Pueblo Esther that combines café, wine, simple gastronomy and curated products inside a concept with local identity and contemporary aesthetics.

It should be understood as:
- a mercato de encuentro
- a café and wine destination
- a social place
- a neighborhood brand with strong visual identity
- a business that mixes hospitality and retail

It is not only a food service website.
It is a brand experience website with practical layers.

---

# 2. Business Identity

Lombardo brings together:

- café de especialidad
- desayunos y meriendas
- pastelería
- vinos y vinos por copa
- cerveza tirada y sidra tirada
- tragos clásicos
- almuerzos simples
- té en hebras
- eventos y experiencias
- retail curated products
- future e-commerce
- future memberships / club

The business sits between:
hospitality + specialty retail + community brand.

---

# 3. Brand Positioning

Lombardo must be perceived as:

- warm
- authentic
- memorable
- accessible
- local
- aesthetically curated
- different from a traditional café
- different from a cold wine shop

It should feel closer to:
- a modern local meeting point
- a wine and coffee concept with style
- a place to return to
- a destination in Pueblo Esther

---

# 4. Strategic Website Purpose

The website should:

- communicate what Lombardo is clearly
- show the atmosphere and identity
- make the menu accessible
- promote café and wine equally
- present experiences and events
- support Club Lombardo
- make contact and location easy
- prepare for future e-commerce expansion

This is not only a brochure site.
It is a strategic business site.

---

# 5. Primary Business Goals

Main goals:
- attract in-person visits
- reinforce brand perception
- show the offer clearly
- increase discovery through digital presence
- support social media traffic
- convert interest into visits, messages and event participation

Secondary goals:
- prepare for online sales
- build a loyalty ecosystem
- present future categories and retail expansion
- support special experiences and memberships

---

# 6. User Goals

A user should be able to:
- understand the concept fast
- see the menu easily
- know what coffee options exist
- know what wine options and experiences exist
- discover events
- understand Club Lombardo
- find the location
- open WhatsApp quickly
- access Instagram
- perceive the business as high quality but approachable

---

# 7. Key User Segments

## Segment 1 — Local habitual visitor
Lives in or near Pueblo Esther.
Looks for a place with identity, quality and comfort.

## Segment 2 — Weekend / visiting audience
Comes from nearby areas and values atmosphere and aesthetics.
May arrive through Instagram or recommendations.

## Segment 3 — Social / aspirational customer
Interested in coffee, wine, visuals, friend meetups and “something different.”

## Segment 4 — Gift / product / curated retail buyer
Interested in wines, delicatessen, gifts and selected products.

## Segment 5 — Experience-driven customer
Interested in catas, events, after office, club benefits and special moments.

---

# 8. Brand Experience Priorities

The website must balance:

1. Identity
2. Atmosphere
3. Practical information
4. Offer clarity
5. Growth potential

This means:
- beautiful but usable
- editorial but not confusing
- practical but not cold

---

# 9. Why the Menu Matters

The menu is not secondary.
It is one of the main conversion tools.

Users must quickly understand:
- what Lombardo serves
- what kind of coffee and wine experience it offers
- how breakfast, merienda and happy hour fit into the concept

The digital menu must therefore be:
- clear
- mobile friendly
- easy to update
- visually on-brand

---

# 10. Why Club Lombardo Matters

Club Lombardo is not just an extra section.
It represents a future loyalty and membership channel.

The website should treat it as:
- a branded benefit ecosystem
- a way to deepen engagement
- a space for discounts, catas and exclusive experiences

This makes the site more than informational.

---

# 11. Why E-commerce Must Be Prepared Now

Even if the online store is not active yet, the architecture should already allow future growth.

Future store possibilities include:
- wines
- delicatessen
- conservas
- fiambres
- carnes envasadas
- gifts
- memberships
- event tickets
- curated experiences

The UI must include a future-ready placeholder, category logic and scalable navigation.

---

# 12. Contact and Location Are Critical

Location, map and direct communication are essential.
Users should never have to search hard for:
- the address
- how to get there
- WhatsApp
- Instagram
- hours

The website must privilege practical conversion elements.

---

# 13. Content Priorities

The most important visible content areas are:

- Hero / identity
- What Lombardo is
- Carta digital
- Café
- Vinos
- Experiencias
- Eventos
- Club Lombardo
- Galería
- Ubicación / contacto

These must all exist clearly.

---

# 14. Long-Term Vision

Lombardo can evolve into a broader ecosystem:
- local landmark brand
- curated retail destination
- event space
- wine club
- digital storefront
- content and social identity hub

The website should therefore be designed as a flexible foundation, not a closed one-page artifact.

---

# 15. Final Strategic Principle

The Lombardo website should make people think:

- this place has something special
- this is easy to understand
- I want to go
- I know what they offer
- I know how to contact them
- I can imagine coming back
